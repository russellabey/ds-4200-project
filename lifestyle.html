Cigarettes Per Day vs. Lifestyle Factors

This heatmap explores how daily cigarette consumption interacts with other lifestyle variables, such as exercise, BMI, diet quality, and alcohol use, to influence lung cancer risk. Darker red cells indicate a higher proportion of patients at elevated risk within that combination of values.

Across all four comparisons, a clear pattern emerges: risk intensifies as cigarette consumption increases, regardless of the lifestyle factor on the y-axis. However, the data also reveals interaction effects worth noting. The darkest cells aren't just about smoking volume; they also tend to involve less exercise, higher BMI, and poorer diet, suggesting that lifestyle factors stack on top of each other to push risk higher. While alcohol use does show higher risk at greater smoking levels, the relationship is patchier and less defined than the other factors, suggesting it plays a less consistent role in elevating lung cancer risk.

Factors in control vs. factors out of control

This bar chart highlights how different factors can influence a person's chance of having cancer. This visualization specficially highlights how factors in control match up against factors that a person cannot control. There is a stark idea that shows through this. There are many things that a person can change in their lifestyle to reduce their change of lung cancer. However, there are still many factors that a person should be aware of to be checked for lung cancer.
